OVERVIEW
Kean University athletics is the official intercollegiate athletics program of Kean University. The official site identifies the program as the Official Athletics Site of the Kean University Cougars. The athletics nickname is Cougars. School colors are Navy, White, and Columbia Blue. Kean University was founded in 1855. Reported enrollment on the athletics quick facts page is 16,019. The athletics department phone is (908) 737-0600. Athletics is affiliated with NCAA Division III.

EAST CAMPUS DETAILS
The East Campus has long served as the home of the men's and women's soccer programs. The grass soccer stadium is described as having two practice fields and a 1,200-seat capacity. The East Campus site notes that it has hosted NCAA Tournament games, including the 1992 Men's Division III National Championship won by Kean. The East Campus gymnasium, named the Jacqueline Towns Court, is described as the primary practice facility for the men's volleyball program. The gym can be configured for two practice volleyball courts and two practice basketball courts. The facility also includes locker rooms and a second-floor fitness area with weight and cardio space for graduate students, faculty, and staff.

D'ANGOLA GYMNASIUM AND FITNESS / POOL DETAILS
D'Angola Gymnasium houses the athletic training and rehabilitation center. The page states the university's main fitness center was renovated and expanded in 2023. It includes Life Fitness weight training machines, free weights, and more than 40 pieces of cardio equipment. The university pool is located inside D'Angola and has six swimming lanes. The pool page notes a 2021 renovation including a new deck surface, starting blocks, and repainting. D'Angola also houses Physical Education department offices and classroom / utility space.

RECREATION AND INTRAMURALS
Kean also operates a Recreation and Intramural Department. Its mission statement says it promotes clean and safe athletic facilities including Harwood Arena, D'Angola Gymnasium, Carole Hynes Field House, Alumni Stadium, and East Campus Fitness Center. The department states that it supports mental, physical, and social wellness and prioritizes free fitness classes and intramurals for students, faculty, and staff.

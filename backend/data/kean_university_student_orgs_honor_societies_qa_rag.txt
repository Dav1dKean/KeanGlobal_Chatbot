CHUNK 1
QUESTION: What are the main ways students get involved at Kean University?
ANSWER: Kean University supports student involvement through Student Life, the Center for Leadership and Service, recognized student organizations, student governments, Greek Life, honor societies, and CougarLink. Student Life presents these areas as major pathways for involvement and campus engagement.

==================================================
CHUNK 2
QUESTION: How many student organizations does Kean University have?
ANSWER: Official Kean pages use more than one count. The Student Life page says students can get involved through more than 160 student organizations. The student group recognition page says there are more than 120 student groups at Kean University. Because both numbers appear on official Kean pages, both should be preserved in a RAG system instead of forcing a single value.

==================================================
CHUNK 3
QUESTION: What is CougarLink at Kean University?
ANSWER: CougarLink is Kean University's online student group management and co-curricular involvement tracking system. Students are directed to CougarLink to search for active groups, explore involvement opportunities, and track extracurricular engagement. Older Kean event-planning guidance also describes CougarLink as the place where student groups submit event requests and activity information.

==================================================
CHUNK 4
QUESTION: Which office handles student group recognition at Kean University?
ANSWER: The Center for Leadership and Service handles student group recognition. Kean states that the Center for Leadership and Service, through the auspices of the Vice President for Student Affairs, is responsible for student group recognition and related development support.

==================================================
CHUNK 5
QUESTION: What types of student groups are officially recognized by Kean University?
ANSWER: Kean's recognition materials identify four broad categories of recognized student groups: Greek organizations, honor societies, student governments, and student clubs and organizations.

==================================================
CHUNK 6
QUESTION: Does the Center for Leadership and Service approve athletic clubs?
ANSWER: No. Kean states that athletic clubs or other high-risk clubs are not administered by the Center for Leadership and Service, and applications for recognition through that office will be denied.

==================================================
CHUNK 7
QUESTION: What is one important requirement for a recognized student organization at Kean?
ANSWER: Kean's Student Group Recognition Policy states that a group must have a full-time Kean University faculty or staff advisor who is appointed for a one-year term and approved by the Center for Leadership and Service.

==================================================
CHUNK 8
QUESTION: What support does Kean provide to recognized student groups?
ANSWER: The Center for Leadership and Service lists multiple support resources for recognized groups, including student group locker space, help with room reservations, assistance with student group forms, assistance with student government paperwork, CougarLink support, student group consulting, a student group and leadership resource library, student group development, and workshops and training.

==================================================
CHUNK 9
QUESTION: What is Greek Life at Kean University?
ANSWER: Kean states that Greek Life empowers members of Greek-letter organizations to promote and preserve the ideals of scholarship, service, leadership, campus involvement, and brotherhood or sisterhood. Kean also says Greek chapters enhance the quality of life for students on and off campus by providing opportunities for meaningful involvement and growth.

==================================================
CHUNK 10
QUESTION: What is the role of the Greek Senate at Kean University?
ANSWER: Kean states that the Greek Senate is advised and supported by the Center for Leadership and Service. It promotes cooperation among Greek organizations through activities and programs and serves as a governing body that develops standards, policies, and procedures relating to Greek affairs.

==================================================
CHUNK 11
QUESTION: Where can students contact Greek Life at Kean University?
ANSWER: Kean's Greek Life page says the Greek Senate can be reached through the Center for Leadership and Service, located in Miron Student Center, Room 219, or by email at greeks@kean.edu.

==================================================
CHUNK 12
QUESTION: Does Kean University hold an event for students interested in Greek organizations?
ANSWER: Yes. Kean says a Meet the Greeks event is held at the beginning of each fall and spring semester so interested students can learn about Greek-letter organizations and involvement opportunities.

CHUNK 13
QUESTION: What national sororities are specifically mentioned on Kean's Greek Life page?
ANSWER: The Greek Life page states that the Panhellenic Association at Kean promotes unity among national sororities and currently lists Delta Phi Epsilon and Theta Phi Alpha.

CHUNK 14
QUESTION: What are the main purposes of fraternities and sororities at Kean University?
ANSWER: Kean lists several purposes for fraternities and sororities: Greek identity and tradition, leadership development, scholastic support, diversified social programming, and community involvement and service.

CHUNK 15
QUESTION: What are honor societies at Kean University?
ANSWER: Kean states that academic achievement is recognized by election during the junior or senior year to one of the honor societies established at the university. Qualified candidates receive notification of eligibility and an invitation to membership directly from the local chapter of the sponsoring honor society.

CHUNK 21
QUESTION: What are sample eligibility examples for Kean honor societies?
ANSWER: Kean maintains honor society information pages with sample eligibility notes. Examples visible there include Phi Alpha Theta requiring a 3.3 overall GPA, 45 credits completed, at least 12 credits in History at Kean, and History as a first or second major. The same information set describes Mu Kappa Tau as requiring at least 50 percent of coursework toward the marketing major, at least a 3.25 GPA, and top 10 percent class standing. The Kean Upsilon Pi Epsilon entry says undergraduate candidates in computing and information disciplines must complete at least 60 semester hours and have at least a 3.45 GPA, while graduate candidates must complete at least 9 semester hours of graduate credit and have at least a 3.45 GPA. These examples should be checked against the current official page or chapter contact because chapter requirements can change.

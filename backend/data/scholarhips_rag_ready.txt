# Scholarship Services
## About
The mission of the Office of Scholarship Services is to help support the academic excellence of prospective and current deserving Kean University students through philanthropic scholarship support. The Office is dedicated to providing financial access to a world-class education, as well as upholding ethical standards of scholarship dissemination and assessment.

The Office of Scholarship Servicesfunctions within the scope of the University in their missions to strategically promote and grow the pool of available scholarships and further the advancement of scholarship recipients as they move beyond Kean University.
Kean University offers a number of scholarships to help qualified undergraduate and graduate students of every race/ethnicity, gender, and creed meet the financial requirements of attending college.
All students should complete a Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) application to assist our Office in confirming their scholarship eligibility. Completing this application may also increase your funding through grants and other types of financial aid. Students should go to Keans How to Apply for Financial Aid web page athttps://www.kean.edu/offices/financial-aid/how-apply-financial-aidfor instructions on how to apply forFinancial Aid, as well as on how to complete a FAFSA application.

## Merit Scholarships
Kean University offers a comprehensive array of merit- and need-based scholarships for incoming freshmen. Freshmen and transfer applicants are automatically considered for merit scholarships as part of their complete application for admission, no separate application is required.
For priority consideration for the William Livingston Scholarship, applicants must submit acomplete application by February 1.
Merit scholarships will continue to be awarded through June 1. Incoming freshmen and transfer students must complete their applications byMay 30to be considered for all other merit awards.
## New Freshmen Merit Scholarships
These awards are given based on alternate indicators including, but not limited to courses taken, academic rigor, class rankwith a primary focus on high school (unweighted) GPA and standardized exams (SAT, ACT) if applicable. Only recent high school graduates primarily admitted as full-time freshmen are eligible.
These scholarships are renewable for up to four years (8 semesters) of consecutive undergraduate study.To renew your scholarship award you must file the FAFSA, maintain full-time enrollment, and maintain a minimumof a cumulative 3.0 GPA.To ensure that you continue to meet the eligibility requirements, this information will be reviewed at the end of each semester by the Office of Scholarship Services. If the requirements are not met at the end of the academic year, your Merit scholarship will be canceledand cannot be reinstated. In addition, it should be noted thatwe areunable to accommodate scholarship appeals.
Please note that merit awards cannot be combined. Scholarship awards have no cash value and we reserve the right to reduce the scholarship amount if the total of all federal, state, and other grant/scholarship awards exceed direct costs defined as tuition, university fees, room and board, if applicable.
There are six named levels of merit scholarships and recognition that range from $500 to full-in-state tuition.

## TRANSFER MERIT SCHOLARSHIP
The Transfer Merit Scholarship is a $1,000 one-time scholarship is awarded based on academic performance from applicant's prior institution of higher education, with 3.0 cumulative GPA and either an associate's degree or comparable coursework totaling a minimum of 60 credits, as determined by the Office of Admissions.
This scholarship is contingent on full-time (minimum of 12 credits) enrollment and does not apply to students who have already earned a bachelor's degree.
In addition, the transfer merit scholarship award has no cash value and we reserve the right to reduce the scholarship amount if the total of all federal, state, and other grant/scholarship awards exceed direct costs defined as tuition, university fees, on-campus room and board, if applicable.

## PHI THETA KAPPA (PTK) SCHOLARSHIP
The PTK Scholarship is a one-time award offered for the first year of study for fall start term students. For students starting in the spring start term, the PTK Scholarship is only awarded in the spring. To qualify, candidates are required to provide theOffice of Transfer Admissionsproofof organizational membership as well as final official transcripts that indicatedegree conferral.
NOTE:If you are in your final semester, you will be tentativelyawarded the PTK Scholarship until Admissions receivesyour final official transcript with your degree conferred.
Know thatthere are two tiers for the PTK Scholarshipbased on full or part time study. Students pursuing full time study (minimum of 12 credits)receive atotal annual amount of $2000($1000 towards each semester,fall and spring) Students studying part time receive a total annual award of $1000($500towards each semester,fall and spring).

## Helpful Tips
- One single application will enable you to be considered for any scholarship that matches your academic history, program, interests, and other criteria.
- Be sure to answer each question in the application. The more complete your application, the better your chances of getting matched.
- Write a well written thoughtful response to the writing prompt. Take this opportunity to introduce yourself to the Scholarship Committee.
- Plan your time. As part of the application, it is highly suggested that you submit a letter of recommendation. Request this from an individual who will be able to provide insight as to who you are and some of your success stories. Your recommendation should come from someone other than a family member, such as a former teacher or academic adviser or someone from a volunteer/community services organization. Be sure to provide ample lead time to ensure you are conscientious of their busy schedules.

Kean University understands that there may be financial challenges faced by full-time undergraduate students that can pose a barrier to completion and graduation. This scholarship is meant to address those challenges by awarding up to $3,000 to cover a balance or up to two summer courses to students who will graduate by the end of the academic year. A limited number of these awards are available annually. Candidacy is reviewed and determined by Kean University and may be based on information from the FAFSA.

Eligibility Criteria are as follows:
1. Full-time undergraduate students with 90 credits or more, pledging to graduate within the academic year (January, May, or August).
2. Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) of $100,000 or less. This information has been entered on the FAFSA and will be confirmed by the Office of Scholarship Services.
3. A gap exists between the financial aid received and direct costs.
4. An application for graduation has been filed before receiving the award.
5. FAFSA filing required, except in special circumstances.

Please note:
FAFSA filing is not required for undocumented students, international students, or special circumstances. In addition, scholarship awards have no cash value, and we reserve the right to reduce the scholarship amount if the total of all federal, state, and other grant/scholarship awards exceed direct costs defined as tuition, university fees, on-campus room and board, if applicable.

Kean University's affordable world-class education is now even more accessible to the region's best and brightest students. Kean has the most affordable tuition at a comprehensive university in New Jersey, and our graduates enter the workforce with less debt than the national average. We are able to achieve this by ensuring that our students are empowered through financial aid and university-funded awards, such as the Climb to Success Scholarship.

Best of all, other than the application for admission, the only application necessary for consideration is the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).

To secure a Climb to Success Scholarship offer, the enrollment deposit and the FAFSA application must be received by May 1.In addition, there may be one-time awards granted to continuing and/or transfer students on a case-by-case basis based on availability of residual funds.

What to do if you have been awarded an external scholarship?
## Remember:
- Please have check made payable to "Kean University"
- Include the student's full name and ID# on the check
- Be sure the funding entity or bank is on the check
- When possible include a letter from the donor or foundation Indicating the semester and year to which the funds are to be applied
- Keep copies of checks and letter for your personal file
Know that the University reserves the right to make any adjustments to need-based aid as a result of receiving external scholarships.
Checks must be sent to:
## Kean University
Office of Student Accounting
## Administration Building, Floor 3
## 1000 Morris Avenue
Union, NJ 07083
## Note:
In some cases a check will follow the notification. If this is the case for you, please email (scholarships@kean.edu) Scholarship Services a copy of the award letter as well as any accompanying information specifying the funding entity's guidelines. This way, a record of the pending award will be reflected in the student's KeanWise account. Once the payment is received, funds will be applied to the student account.
Depending on the external donor's specifications, an external scholarship can sometimes be moved across semesters to cover a previous or forthcoming balance. Donor approval must be granted in these situations, and the University reserves the right to adjust the funds from an external scholarship to coincide with any financial aid that a student may already be receiving. In the event that an external scholarship exceeds the cost of attendance, and again per the donor's criteria, a refund check could be issued. In these cases, the recipient is strongly encouraged to apply this refund to a forthcoming tuition payment or books.
It is the student's responsibility to follow up with donors on all external scholarship issues.
Know that we cannot process checks made out directly to a student, nor can we accept a check which lists a student as the co-payee.
Please emailscholarships@kean.eduwith any questions about the external scholarship process.

Securing your Scholarship
A formal thank-you letter is one of the requirements in receiving a Kean University Foundation scholarship. Our donors would like to hear how scholarship funding is making a difference in the lives of students and how it will help them persist academically and personally. For students, this is a great way to share your gratitude and appreciation for being selected. Please keep in mind that you also need to toreview the scholarship agreementandaccept the award. These steps are all to be completed on the Scholarship Manager website,Login Here.
## Tips:
- Be formal and professional.
- Introduce yourself and which scholarship you are writing on behalf.
- Share your personal story.
- Discuss your academic journey and include your future career and educational plans
- What is the impact of receiving this scholarship for you and/or your family?
- Close with a sincere thank you and make a commitment to strive for excellence in your studies and service to others.
If further assistance is required in writing the letter, Kean students can use theWriting Centeras a resource.

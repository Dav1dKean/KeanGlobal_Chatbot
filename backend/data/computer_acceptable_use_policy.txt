KEAN UNIVERSITY
COMPUTER RELATED ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY

PREAMBLE
Kean University is committed to protecting and ensuring the availability and integrity of its data, information systems, and computer networks through appropriate security measures and controls.

All users share responsibility for maintaining security and must follow established guidelines.

OVERVIEW
This policy outlines acceptable use of University information systems and equipment. Improper use may expose the University to risks including:
- Security breaches
- Network compromise
- Virus attacks
- Legal liability

The University may monitor and record system usage. Users expressly consent to monitoring. Evidence of criminal activity may be referred to law enforcement.

AUTHORIZED USERS
University computer resources are restricted to:
- Registered students
- Faculty
- Staff

Violations may result in:
- Suspension of computer privileges
- Referral to Student Accountability (students)
- Referral to Human Resources (employees)
- Criminal prosecution where applicable

GENERAL ACCEPTABLE USE POLICIES

Users MUST NOT:

1. Use an ID not assigned to them.
2. Share passwords or access credentials.
3. Attempt to obtain another user’s password.
4. Disguise account identity.
5. Gain unauthorized access to remote computers.
6. Disrupt network or system operations.
7. Install or distribute malicious software (viruses, worms, Trojan horses).
8. Circumvent security protections.
9. Perform port scanning or security scanning.
10. Violate copyright, intellectual property, or software licenses.
11. Send spam, chain letters, or excessive mass emails.
12. Harass others through threatening, abusive, or offensive messages.
13. Monitor, read, copy, or alter another user's data without permission.
14. Use university systems for personal financial gain.
15. Place prohibited content on university systems.

PROHIBITED CONTENT
The following may not be stored or distributed on University systems:
- Infringing material
- Abusive or sexually offensive material
- Pornographic content
- Pirated software
- Destructive software
- Libelous statements
- Commercial advertisements

USE OF GENERATIVE AI TOOLS

Kean University supports responsible AI use but recognizes security and intellectual property risks.

GENERAL AI REQUIREMENTS:
- AI use must be disclosed when assisting with tasks.
- AI-generated content must be fact-checked.
- AI tools may not be used to make employment decisions.
- Confidential or sensitive information must never be input into unauthorized AI systems.

SANCTIONED AI TOOL (As of June 23, 2025):
- Microsoft Copilot Chat (via Microsoft Teams or Office.com)

ChatGPT or other generative AI tools are NOT authorized for University business unless officially approved.

RESTRICTIONS FOR NON-SANCTIONED AI TOOLS:
Users must NOT input:
- Personal information
- Student records subject to FERPA
- Protected health information
- Personnel records
- Passwords or ID numbers
- Confidential University information
- The name of Kean University or departments

AI tools may not be used for:
- Illegal or fraudulent activities
- Recording meetings without consent
- Personnel or sensitive matter transcription

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination.

EXTERNAL NETWORK USE
Users connecting to external networks must comply with:
- Kean University policies
- Policies of the external network

KEAN UNIVERSITY
ACADEMIC INTEGRITY POLICY
Revised Fall 2024
Approved by the Kean University Board of Trustees on September 16, 2024

------------------------------------------------------------
1. PREFACE
------------------------------------------------------------

Kean University is committed to creating an environment where academic integrity is supported and academic dishonesty is not tolerated.

All members of the Kean University community must understand:
- What constitutes academic integrity
- How to prevent academic dishonesty
- What sanctions are imposed for academic dishonesty
- What consequences result from such sanctions
- What process is used to impose those sanctions

All members of the Kean community must:
- Represent their identity truthfully
- Protect academic materials from unauthorized access
- Protect passwords and access codes
- Properly acknowledge the work of others
- Report data and sources accurately
- Refuse to participate in academic dishonesty
- Follow professional and ethical codes of their discipline
- Truthfully represent mastery of material

------------------------------------------------------------
2. ACADEMIC INTEGRITY PRINCIPLES AND VALUES
------------------------------------------------------------

Kean University upholds five fundamental values:

1. Honesty – Intellectual and personal honesty in learning, teaching, research, and service.
2. Trust – A climate of mutual trust and respect.
3. Fairness – Clear standards, practices, and procedures.
4. Respect – Recognition of diverse ideas and perspectives.
5. Responsibility – Personal accountability and action in the face of wrongdoing.

All academic work must reflect a person’s own efforts. Collaboration, including the use of Artificial Intelligence, must be acknowledged.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. ACADEMIC INTEGRITY CATEGORIES
------------------------------------------------------------

Academic integrity responsibilities fall into four areas:

1. Mastery of Material
   Truthful representation of knowledge and understanding.

2. Representation of Sources
   Complete and accurate acknowledgment of others’ work.

3. Truthful Submission of Work
   Honest presentation of data, research, and creative works.

4. Access and Use of Resources
   Authorized use of copyrighted and licensed materials.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE EXPECTATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Artificial Intelligence (AI) includes generative tools such as ChatGPT, GPT-4, DALL-E, and similar systems.

Faculty specify in their course syllabi how AI may be used.

University-wide AI guidelines:

- Originality: AI-generated content cannot be presented as original student work.
- Citation: AI-generated content must be cited and attributed.
- Integrity: Students are responsible for verifying accuracy of AI-generated content.
- Consequences: Improper AI use may result in failing the assignment, failing the course, or disciplinary action.

Failure to seek clarification about AI usage is not a valid excuse for violations.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. CATEGORIES OF VIOLATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Violations fall into four categories:

1. Cheating
   Misrepresentation of mastery of material.

2. Plagiarism
   Presenting another’s words, ideas, or data as one’s own.

3. Fabrication
   Inventing information or falsifying data.

4. Academic Misconduct
   Any other form of academic dishonesty, including assisting others.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. CLASSIFICATION OF VIOLATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Violations are classified into four levels:

LEVEL ONE
- Minor violations due to inexperience
- Activity counts for less than 10% of course grade
Sanction: Make-up work or no credit on assignment

LEVEL TWO
- More serious violations
- Activity counts for less than 25% of course grade
Sanction: Failing grade on assignment
Report filed with Office of the Provost

LEVEL THREE
- Repeat Level Two or major violation
- Activity counts for 25% or more of course grade
Sanction: Probation or suspension for one semester
Failing grade in course

LEVEL FOUR
- Most serious violations
- May involve legal violations or thesis/dissertation misconduct
Sanction: Expulsion and permanent dismissal notation

------------------------------------------------------------
7. FRAUD AND PURCHASED PAPERS
------------------------------------------------------------

Purchasing or submitting work prepared by another individual or company is fraud and illegal under New Jersey law.

Penalties may include fines and University disciplinary action.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. COURSE WITHDRAWAL
------------------------------------------------------------

Withdrawing from a course does not eliminate responsibility for academic integrity violations. If found responsible, a failing grade may still be assigned.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. REPORTING AND APPEALS PROCESS
------------------------------------------------------------

Level 2, 3, and 4 violations must be reported using the Academic Integrity Violations Report (AIVR).

Students receive official notification through the University system.

For Level 3 and 4 violations:
- Sanctions are determined by the College Dean.
- Students may appeal within 10 calendar days.
- Appeals are reviewed by the University Appeals Board (UAB).

------------------------------------------------------------
10. UNIVERSITY APPEALS BOARD (UAB)
------------------------------------------------------------

The UAB:
- Reviews student appeals
- Includes faculty, staff, students, and administrators
- Requires quorum to make decisions
- May uphold, modify, or dismiss charges

Decisions may only be appealed on procedural or substantive error grounds.

------------------------------------------------------------
11. DISCIPLINARY RECORDS
------------------------------------------------------------

Disciplinary records are maintained:
- Seven years after last attendance
- Permanently for suspensions, expulsions, and appeals

FERPA governs student educational record access and disclosure.

KEAN UNIVERSITY
FAMILY EDUCATIONAL RIGHTS AND PRIVACY ACT (FERPA)

OVERVIEW
The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) grants students certain rights, privileges, and protections regarding their education records maintained by Kean University.

DEFINITION OF A STUDENT
A student is defined as a person taking classes at any campus or site of Kean University.
A person becomes a student when they attend their first class of the semester.

If the individual is fully matriculated or at least 18 years old by the first day of the first semester, FERPA rights belong to the student. Otherwise, rights belong to the parent or legal guardian until the student turns 18 or matriculates.

EDUCATION RECORDS
Education records include records directly related to a student, including but not limited to:
- Admissions records
- Academic progress
- Registrar records
- Health records
- Financial aid records
- Student accounting records
- Teacher education records
- Career placement records
- Disciplinary records

EXCLUSIONS FROM EDUCATION RECORDS
The following are not considered education records:
- Personal notes kept in sole possession of faculty or staff
- Peer-graded assignments before being recorded
- Treatment records maintained by medical professionals
- Law enforcement records
- Alumni records unrelated to student attendance
- Financial information submitted by parents
- Employment records unrelated to student status

RIGHT TO INSPECT RECORDS
Students have the right to inspect and review their education records within 45 days of submitting a written request to the University Registrar.

RIGHT TO REQUEST AMENDMENT
Students may request corrections to inaccurate or misleading information in their records. If denied, students have the right to a hearing.

DISCLOSURE WITHOUT CONSENT
The University may disclose education records without student consent in certain circumstances, including:
- School officials with legitimate educational interest
- Other institutions where a student seeks enrollment
- Accrediting organizations
- Financial aid agencies
- Compliance with judicial orders or subpoenas
- Health and safety emergencies
- Parents of dependent students (tax purposes)

DIRECTORY INFORMATION
Kean University defines directory information as:
- Student name
- Student ID number (excluding password)
- Email address
- Program of study
- Enrollment status
- Honors and awards
- Degrees received
- Participation in activities
- Athletic team information

Students may request to withhold directory information by submitting a written request to the Office of the Registrar by the end of the second week of the semester.

RIGHT TO FILE A COMPLAINT

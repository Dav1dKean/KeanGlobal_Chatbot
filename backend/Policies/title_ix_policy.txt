KEAN UNIVERSITY
TITLE IX POLICY – SEXUAL HARASSMENT AND MISCONDUCT

POLICY STATEMENT
Kean University is committed to providing an environment free from discrimination based on sex, including sexual harassment, sexual assault, dating violence, domestic violence, and stalking.

This policy complies with Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972.

PROHIBITED CONDUCT
Prohibited behaviors include:
- Sexual harassment
- Sexual assault
- Non-consensual sexual contact
- Dating violence
- Domestic violence
- Stalking
- Retaliation against individuals who report misconduct

DEFINITION OF SEXUAL HARASSMENT
Sexual harassment includes conduct on the basis of sex that:
- Is unwelcome
- Is severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive
- Denies equal access to education

REPORTING OPTIONS
Students and employees may report Title IX violations to:
- The Title IX Coordinator
- University Police
- Appropriate University officials

The University encourages prompt reporting.

INVESTIGATION PROCESS
- Reports will be reviewed promptly.
- A fair and impartial investigation will be conducted.
- Both parties will have equal opportunity to present evidence.
- A written determination will be issued.

INTERIM MEASURES
The University may implement supportive measures, including:
- No-contact directives
- Academic accommodations
- Housing adjustments
- Counseling support

RETALIATION
Retaliation against individuals who report misconduct or participate in investigations is strictly prohibited.

CONFIDENTIAL RESOURCES
Confidential support may be available through:
- Counseling services
- Health services

CONTACT INFORMATION
For Title IX matters, contact the Kean University Title IX Coordinator.
